# Product Model

**Version:** v1 - pre-research hypotheses
**Status:** Awaiting validation in Phases 3 and 4

---

## 1. Product Objectives

| # | Objective | Metric | Target (hypothesis) |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | Build a trusted path from goal to product for non-expert buyers | Guided quiz completion rate; first-purchase conversion from quiz | 40% quiz completion, 20% quiz-to-purchase |
| 2 | Establish the coach/gym ordering channel as primary revenue driver | % of GMV from coach accounts; average coach order value | 40% of GMV from coach accounts within 6 months |
| 3 | Drive repeat purchases and reduce catalog abandonment | 30-day repeat purchase rate; cart abandonment rate | 35% repeat rate, <60% abandonment |
| 4 | Build trust and reduce uncertainty for the safety-sensitive buyer | Product page time spent; review/trust signal engagement; return rate | <5% return rate, trust element engagement on >60% of sessions |

## 2. Audience Segments

### Segment 1: Coaches, Gyms, Team Managers (Primary)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Age | 25-45 |
| Profile | Professional/semi-pro coaches, gym owners, team managers. Purchase for 5-30+ athletes. Know products well. Act as trust proxy for clients. |
| Motivation | Serve athletes reliably. Maintain professional credibility. Save time on bulk ordering. |
| Pain | Existing stores built for solo B2C. Ordering for multiple people with different needs is slow and error-prone. No bulk/coach account tools. |
| JTBD | When I am ordering supplements for my clients, I want to build multi-client orders in one session, so that I can serve my athletes reliably and maintain their trust in my professional recommendations. |
| Priority | PRIMARY - this channel brings multiple repeat customers per account, high AOV, and is underserved by current UA market |

### Segment 2: Non-Expert Beginners (Secondary)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Age | 18-35 |
| Profile | Starting a fitness journey or targeting a specific outcome (fat loss, muscle gain, energy, recovery). No or minimal sport nutrition knowledge. Referred by coach or found via social/search. |
| Motivation | Achieve a fitness goal safely. Get confident, clear direction without having to research everything. |
| Pain | Enormous catalog, unfamiliar terminology, fear of side effects or wrong products, no trusted source to verify. |
| JTBD | When I want to improve my body or performance but feel overwhelmed by the catalog, I want to answer a few questions and get a clear, safe product set, so that I can start confidently without needing to become an expert first. |
| Priority | SECONDARY - high volume but lower AOV and higher guidance cost; becomes less costly to serve as trust systems mature |

### Segment 3: Experienced Regulars ("Always in Stock") (Supporting / Later)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Age | 22-40 |
| Profile | Experienced buyers with established supplement routines. Know exactly what they want. Time-poor. Loyal to a store they trust. |
| Motivation | Never run out of staples. Minimize decision effort for repeat purchases. |
| Pain | Re-navigating the catalog every time. Out-of-stock surprises. No reminder or auto-reorder system. |
| JTBD | When I am running low on my staple products, I want to reorder in one or two taps, so that I never run out and don't waste mental energy on repeat decisions. |
| Priority | SUPPORTING/LATER - valuable for retention and LTV, but not the acquisition wedge. Reorder as free convenience in MVP. |

## 3. AIDA per Segment

### Segment 1: Coaches / Gyms

| AIDA Stage | Channel | Message | Mechanic |
|------------|---------|---------|----------|
| Attention | Instagram/TikTok for coaches, word-of-mouth within gym communities, direct outreach to gym owners | "Ordering for your athletes? There is a better way." | Short-form video showing the pain of current ordering process vs. Stack's multi-client cart |
| Interest | Landing page for coaches, dedicated coach section in navigation | "One session, all your clients covered. Bulk pricing, saved profiles, fast reorder." | Coach account demo, testimonial from a gym owner, pricing calculator |
| Desire | Coach account signup flow, onboarding | "See how much time you save on your first order." | Free trial of coach account features, personalized onboarding call (early stage) |
| Action | Coach registration, first order | "Set up your team in 5 minutes." | Streamlined onboarding, clear first-order CTA, minimal friction to first purchase |

### Segment 2: Non-Expert Beginners

| AIDA Stage | Channel | Message | Mechanic |
|------------|---------|---------|----------|
| Attention | Social (TikTok/Instagram), search ("what supplements to take for X"), referral from coach | "Not sure what to take? Start here." | Goal-based entry point on homepage, search-optimized goal landing pages |
| Interest | Goal quiz, goal-specific landing pages | "Three questions. A safe, clear product plan for your goal." | Interactive quiz with immediate, personalized results |
| Desire | Quiz results page, product pages | "This is exactly what you need and why it is safe." | Clear composition breakdown, dosage guide, origin/certification badge, "approved by coach" social proof |
| Action | Add to cart from quiz results, simplified checkout | "Your starter pack is ready." | Pre-built goal bundle add-to-cart, one-click guest checkout option |

### Segment 3: Regulars

| AIDA Stage | Channel | Message | Mechanic |
|------------|---------|---------|----------|
| Attention | Email/SMS reminder, app notification (web push), account dashboard | "You are probably running low on [product]." | Estimated reorder reminder based on last purchase date and typical cycle |
| Interest | Account dashboard "My Staples" list | "Reorder everything in one tap." | Saved product list with one-tap repeat order |
| Desire | Order history page | "Same as last time. Same price. In stock." | Stock status on saved items, price consistency messaging |
| Action | One-tap reorder | "Done." | Minimal confirmation flow, saved address and payment method |

## 4. AIDA to AARRR Mapping

| AIDA Stage | AARRR Stage | Notes |
|------------|-------------|-------|
| Attention | Acquisition | Paid social, SEO, coach outreach, referral - getting people to the store for the first time |
| Interest | Activation | Goal quiz, product guidance, coach account demo - the moment the user gets value from the store before buying |
| Desire | Activation / Revenue | Product page trust signals, bundle builder, coach tools - converting interest into intent |
| Action | Revenue | First purchase - the conversion event |
| (Post-purchase) | Retention | Reorder mechanics, account, reminders - keeping buyers coming back |
| (Sharing) | Referral | Coach spreading to athletes, social sharing of goals/results, word of mouth |
