## Patterns, Differences, and Gaps

### 3 Common Patterns (across all groups)

1. **Goal buckets are universal, but depth varies dramatically.** Every competitor from belok.ua to Huel uses some form of goal-based navigation. The UA stores use 3-5 static goal categories. The international aspirational players use interactive quizzes, AI advisors, and personalized paths. The mechanism is the same; the execution gap is enormous.

2. **Trust is built differently at different scales.** UA stores rely on physical presence, years in operation, and "100% original" claims. International leaders rely on third-party certifications (NSF, Informed Sport, BRCGS), clinical partnerships, and volume social proof (210k+ reviews). Neither approach is wrong - they reflect different market maturities.

3. **Reorder is an afterthought in Ukraine, a product feature internationally.** Belok has a cumulative discount system. Liki24 has cashback. But MyProtein, Bulk, Thorne, and Huel all have structured subscription models with explicit savings (20%+ off). The "never run out" job is clearly unmet in the Ukrainian market.

### 3 Key Differences

1. **Only belok.ua has a visible coach/wholesale program.** Every other UA hard competitor treats all buyers identically. Belok's wholesale form is a meaningful differentiator, but it is buried at /opt/ and not front-of-site. No UA competitor positions the coach channel as a primary trust signal or community.

2. **No Ukrainian store communicates composition, dosage, or testing in depth.** The SOFT and ASPIRATIONAL groups all invest heavily in ingredient transparency (Thorne's 4 rounds of testing, Bulk's batch-level Informed Sport, Huel's 100+ studies). UA stores say "100% original" and stop there. This is the largest trust communication gap.

3. **Mobile is underdeveloped in UA.** Only belok.ua has a confirmed Android app. Liki24 (SOFT) has a full iOS + Android app with a redesigned UX. No UA sport nutrition store has a confirmed iOS app. International players all have mobile-first experiences with app purchase incentives.

### What is Missing Across All (Our Opportunity Gap)

- **A real interactive goal-to-product path for the Ukrainian market.** The best UA store (Belok) offers 3 static goal buckets. No quiz, no personalization, no conversational guidance. Thorne's Taia, Huel's quiz, Myprotein's 6-goal selector - none of these have a Ukrainian equivalent.
- **Coach ordering as a front-of-site, community-centered feature.** Belok has a wholesale form. No competitor treats coaches as a channel with their own identity, tools, and trust signals.
- **Subscription/auto-delivery in the Ukrainian market.** Completely absent from all UA hard competitors.
- **Third-party quality certification communication.** Belok has an empty certificates page. No UA store explains testing, sourcing, or manufacturing.
- **Per-serving cost parity.** Belok does this well. Other UA competitors do not. A foundational UX feature worth matching.

### 3 Open Questions

1. Are Ukrainian gym coaches reachable primarily through digital channels (Instagram, Telegram) or through in-person/community relationships? This affects coach acquisition strategy significantly.
2. What is the real competitive intensity for goal-based SEO in Ukrainian? Are terms like "спортивне харчування для схуднення" low or medium competition?
3. Do Ukrainian buyers respond to international quality certifications (NSF, Informed Sport) or do they require Ukrainian/EU-specific signals? The market is still developing trust vocabulary.
