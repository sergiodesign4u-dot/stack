# Benchmark: Trust and Guidance for Sport Nutrition Buyers

**Research date:** 2026-06-10
**Focus:** How best-in-class products translate a non-expert's goal into the right, safe product set; build credibility; and make reorder effortless.
**All claims cite sources. Unverified data marked [?].**

---

## 1. Evaluation Criteria (Scale 1-5)

| # | Criterion | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Clarity of first screen** | Does the homepage immediately communicate what the store is for, who it is for, and what to do next? No jargon. One clear path. |
| 2 | **Goal-to-product guidance** | Can a non-expert with a vague goal (lose fat, gain mass, more energy) find the right product set in under 3 clicks without knowing product names? |
| 3 | **Composition and dosage transparency** | Are ingredients, amounts, dosage instructions, and form (powder/tablet/liquid) visible and understandable without navigating away from the product page? |
| 4 | **Origin and certification signals** | Are manufacturing origin, third-party certifications, and testing protocols communicated clearly and prominently? |
| 5 | **Social proof quality** | Is social proof specific, credible, and contextually relevant (not just a star count)? Named experts, real use cases, volume + quality. |
| 6 | **Calm and trustworthy tone** | Does the visual and copy tone feel safe, professional, and unhurried? No countdown timers, no anxiety popups, no aggressive sales mechanics. |
| 7 | **Reorder ease** | Can a returning buyer who knows what they want reorder in 2 taps or fewer from their account or landing page? |
| 8 | **Coach / bulk ordering** | Is there a visible, functional path for a coach or professional ordering for multiple clients? Saved profiles, bulk pricing, account tools. |

## 4. Key Observations per Product

### Myprotein (31/40)
The scale leader. 210,000+ Trustpilot reviews at 4.4 create credibility through volume, and the 6-goal selector (Cardio / Balanced Lifestyle / Get Stronger / Build Muscle / Lose Weight / Elite Performance) is the clearest goal-to-product map in the category - each goal links to a curated product collection. The explicit "we don't spike our protein, ever" statement is a rare, direct anti-fraud signal. However, the homepage is visually loud - multiple competing sale banners make the first impression feel promotional rather than trustworthy. The calm/tone score (3/5) reflects this.
Source: https://www.myprotein.com/, https://us.myprotein.com/c/goal-selector/

### Thorne (36/40)
The trust benchmark. Four rounds of testing (raw material purity, ingredient potency, contaminant absence, shelf life) - communicated clearly on the quality page - is the most rigorous transparency in the comparison. The Taia AI advisor is the most advanced goal-to-product mechanism: conversational, remembers previous sessions, can compare products, identifies overlap, and is grounded in clinical trial data. Physician-reviewed health test results are a unique trust layer. Tone is calm, clinical, and adult. Weaknesses: no coach/team bulk program, and the homepage can feel overwhelming to a first-time user (10+ content sections).
Source: https://www.thorne.com/, https://www.thorne.com/featured/taia, https://www.thorne.com/nsf-certified-for-sport

### Huel (31/40)
The best quiz execution in the comparison. "Which Huel is right for you?" and the "Choose Your Goal" 4-goal page provide dual discovery paths, and the B Corp certification (score 92.1) is the strongest corporate-level trust signal. The "100+ peer-reviewed studies" claim is the most specific and actionable science statement reviewed. Tone is calm and confident. The 30-day taste guarantee is a low-friction risk reversal. Major weakness: no coach or bulk ordering path at all - the product is not designed for professional or volume buyers.
Source: https://huel.com/, https://huel.com/pages/choose-your-goal, https://huel.com/pages/huel-is-now-b-corp-certified

### Bulk (30/40)
The Informed Sport implementation leader. 149 batch-tested products (tested by LGC Sport Science) is the most concrete product-level certification program reviewed. The dedicated Informed Sport range page at /uk/sports-nutrition/informed-sport lets competitive athletes shop with confidence. Bulk Boost (GBP 9.95/year, unlimited free express delivery over GBP 10) is the simplest and most accessible subscription model reviewed - it solves reorder through economic incentive rather than automation. The 4-goal homepage tiles are visually clean and easy to navigate. Weaknesses: no coach/B2B offering, and overall trust depth is below Thorne.
Source: https://bulk.com/, https://www.bulk.com/uk/sports-nutrition/informed-sport, https://help.bulk.com/hc/en-gb/articles/23536492662289-Bulk-Boost

### Liki24 (24/40)
The most relevant local reference. The symptom-based navigation ("Симптоми") is the best goal-to-product mechanism in the Ukrainian market - it maps health concerns to products, not product categories to products. This is the key behavioral insight for Stack: Ukrainian buyers already understand this pattern from pharmacy e-commerce. However, Liki24 is a marketplace aggregator, not a direct seller, so composition/dosage transparency depends on the listed seller. The Qubstudio app redesign (iOS, App Store ID 1577477032) focused on reducing registration friction and simplifying status communication - directly relevant to Stack's onboarding design.
Source: https://liki24.com/, https://qubstudio.com/blog/internship-ux-improvement-of-liki24/, https://apps.apple.com/ua/app/liki24-health-and-beauty/id1577477032

## 5. Top 3 Mechanisms to Carry into Our MVP

### Mechanism 1: The Goal Selector as primary entry point (from Myprotein + Huel)

**What it is exactly:** Myprotein's goal selector page (https://us.myprotein.com/c/goal-selector/) presents 6 goal tiles (Cardio, Balanced Lifestyle, Get Stronger, Build Muscle, Lose Weight, Elite Performance) as the primary discovery path. Huel's "Choose Your Goal" page (https://huel.com/pages/choose-your-goal) uses 4 goals (Lose weight, More protein, Eat healthy, On-the-go) with supporting visuals. Both lead directly to curated product collections, not to filtered catalog pages.

**Where to use it:** Stack's homepage hero and primary navigation. Not as a secondary feature - as the main entry point for a first-time visitor. A beginner who lands on Stack should see their goal before they see a product name.

**Why it works (the psychological mechanism):** It eliminates the paralysis of an undifferentiated catalog. The buyer does not need to know product terminology. They recognize their goal and trust that the store has done the curation work for them. This is precisely the trust transfer that converts an uncertain visitor into a buyer. The goal is the hook; the curated collection is the payload.

### Mechanism 2: Layered trust signals at the product level (from Thorne + Bulk)

**What it is exactly:** Thorne communicates 4 rounds of testing (https://www.thorne.com/nsf-certified-for-sport) on every product: raw material purity, ingredient potency, contaminant absence, shelf life confirmation. Each is explained in plain language. Bulk shows Informed Sport batch certification (https://www.bulk.com/uk/sports-nutrition/informed-sport) per product, linking each certified SKU to the LGC Sport Science test result.

**Where to use it:** Stack's product pages, above the fold. Not as a footnote - as a primary content block. At minimum: origin country flag, certification badge (if applicable), and one-sentence composition summary before price or quantity.

**Why it works:** The buyer's primary doubt at the product page is "is this safe and real?" Price and quantity are secondary to safety when the buyer is uncertain. Answering the safety question first (before asking for money) builds the trust that makes the purchase feel correct rather than risky. This is especially relevant for the Ukrainian market where counterfeit supplements are a real buyer concern.

### Mechanism 3: Symptom/concern-based navigation as secondary discovery path (from Liki24)

**What it is exactly:** Liki24's "Симптоми" navigation category (https://liki24.com/) maps health concerns - not product categories - to relevant products. Examples: "При болю і спазмах" (Pain and Spasms), "Від грипу та застуди" (Cold and Flu). The buyer describes their situation, not their desired product.

**Where to use it:** Stack's guided quiz and search. When a beginner cannot identify their goal in fitness terms ("build muscle" / "lose fat"), offer a secondary path: "What is your main challenge?" with options like "I get tired quickly," "I am losing muscle," "I want to recover faster." Each maps to a product set without requiring sport nutrition vocabulary.

**Why it works:** It meets the buyer where they are. "I get tired quickly" is a more natural self-description than "I need a pre-workout." This path has lower abandonment because it never asks the buyer to translate their lived experience into product jargon. Liki24 proved this works at scale in the Ukrainian market (1M+ customers).

## 6. One Mechanism That Will NOT Work for Our Context

### Subscription-first purchase model (from AG1 / Thorne)

**What it is:** AG1 (https://drinkag1.com/) and Thorne both present the subscription as the primary or default purchase option. AG1's entire pricing architecture is subscription-first - the one-time purchase option is secondary and less visible.

**Why it will NOT work for Stack's context:** Ukrainian e-commerce buyers have significantly lower subscription trust than North American or Western European buyers. Subscription billing in Ukraine is associated with high friction (card enrollment, recurring charges, unclear cancellation) and low consumer protection. The majority of Stack's target buyers - especially the beginner segment - would abandon a product page that leads with subscription over a straightforward "add to cart." Additionally, belok.ua and all other UA competitors use one-time purchase only, meaning subscription is not a established behavior in this market.

For Stack, subscription is a retention mechanic for Phase 2, not an acquisition mechanic for MVP. Reorder reminders via email/SMS achieve the same "never run out" outcome without requiring a subscription commitment at first purchase.
